# Cold Outreach — Week 1 Drafts

**👁️ REVIEW-ONLY** — Tinashe reviews the tone, then personalises the actual numbers (after running PageSpeed on each target) and sends.

**Send channel:** LinkedIn DM if you can find the decision-maker; email to info@ / contact@ as fallback.

**Tone principles (keep these tight):**
- Lead with a specific observation about THEIR site, not a compliment
- Numbers, not adjectives ("5.2 second LCP" not "slow")
- Three findings max — more feels like a pitch
- Close with a no-pressure next step
- Never mention price, ever
- Do NOT say "I'd love to help you" — that's transparent sales language
- Sign off as "Tinashe" not "Tinashe Munyaka — Founder of Origami Digital" (too formal for cold DM)

## Response playbook

**They reply asking questions:**
Answer concretely, send the audit writeup as a PDF attachment. Don't jump to pitching a project.

**They reply positively, want to talk:**
Book a 20-minute call. Come with screenshots, PageSpeed results, and the 3-5 quick-win recommendations. Pitch only if they ask "so what would it cost to fix this?"

**They reply defensively ("we already have a developer"):**
"Totally understand — wasn't suggesting a replacement. If they're across the [LCP / schema / whatever] issue already, great. If not, feel free to forward this note to them. Cheers."

**They ignore:**
ONE follow-up after 10 days. Share the blog post on the topic: "Thought you might find this relevant — https://origami-digital.co.za/journal/signs-website-needs-redesign-2026" or the GEO piece. Then drop it.

## Expected conversion funnel (rough)

- 10 messages sent → 1-2 reply positively → 1 books a call → 0-1 converts to project
- 50 messages over 5 weeks → 5-10 replies → 3-5 calls → 1-3 projects
- Focus on the long game: a "no thanks" this month can become a "we're ready now" in 6 months if you've stayed helpful and un-pushy
