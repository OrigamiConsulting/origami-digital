# Cold Outreach Targets — Week 1

10 SA SMEs identified as prospects for the "website roast" outbound approach (MARKETING-LAUNCH.md Task 10.5). The principle: find businesses with clearly fixable website problems, audit specifically, lead with unsolicited value, no pitch.

**Approach:** Text-based audit-summary email/LinkedIn message first. Add Loom videos for the highest-priority targets only.

**Important — Tinashe verification step BEFORE sending:**
1. Open each target's website
2. Confirm the 3 flagged issues are still present (things change fast — I can't currently verify)
3. Run PageSpeed Insights yourself on their URL, copy the actual numbers
4. Find the right contact on LinkedIn (decision-maker, not generic inbox)

I'm flagging *categories* of real issues that are overwhelmingly common across SA SME sites. On the live sites, you'll almost certainly find at least 2 of the 3 I've flagged per target. Use your judgement.

## Vertical 1 — Law firms (mid-market JHB boutiques)

### 1. Burger Huyser Attorneys
- **URL:** burgerhuyserattorneys.co.za
- **Why target:** Mid-sized boutique, active online, likely serves SME commercial clients who judge them partly on web quality
- **Contact:** Founder/MD — find on LinkedIn
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Mobile LCP likely >3s (heavy WordPress template)
  - Missing LegalService or LocalBusiness schema
  - FAQ section likely without FAQPage schema — so they're invisible when ChatGPT answers "best family lawyer in Johannesburg"

### 2. Barter McKellar

## Vertical 2 — Property developers / estate agents (Gauteng)

### 4. Dogon Group (JHB branches)
- **URL:** dogongroup.com
- **Why target:** Active agency, multiple JHB branches, likely has dated site
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Image-heavy site, likely slow LCP (>4s on mobile)
  - Listing pages probably lack Product/Offer schema
  - No FAQ schema for common buyer questions

### 5. Pam Golding Properties (regional franchisees)
- **URL:** pamgolding.co.za (and sub-sites for specific JHB branches — Sandton, Bedfordview)
- **Why target:** Individual franchisee sites are often worse than the main site — real opportunity at branch level
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Branch microsites typically slow, duplicate content issues
  - Very limited structured data

### 6. A smaller independent developer (research on Google: "property developer Johannesburg")
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Developer sites are famous for having one-page "launch microsites" that never get optimised
  - Often missing contact forms that actually work

## Vertical 3 — Accounting / professional services

### 7. Find a mid-size accounting firm via findanaccountant.co.za (JHB, not the largest)
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Likely dated WordPress site with generic theme
  - Team page probably missing Person schema
  - No blog or insights section — lost SEO + GEO opportunity

### 8. Financial advisory firms (search: "financial planner Sandton")
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Compliance-heavy industry, sites often extremely text-heavy and badly structured
  - FAQ sections without schema
  - Mobile UX often bad

## Vertical 4 — Private healthcare

### 9. Private dental practices (Sandton/Fourways/Bedfordview)
- **How to find:** Google "dentist Sandton", "dental practice Fourways"
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Sites often built by GP-marketing agencies with cookie-cutter templates
  - Booking flows are usually broken or clunky
  - No LocalBusiness/Dentist schema = invisible in AI health queries

### 10. Specialist medical practices (dermatologists, physiotherapists)
- **How to find:** Google "dermatologist Johannesburg private practice"
- **Common findings to verify:**
  - Healthcare sites are often 5+ years old, non-responsive on modern phones
  - No POPIA-compliant contact forms
  - Missing MedicalBusiness / Physician schema
