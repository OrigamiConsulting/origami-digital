## Monday — Educational (schema markup for GEO)

```
Most SA business websites have zero structured data. And it's quietly costing them AI-search visibility.

Structured data (schema markup) is invisible code that tells Google, ChatGPT and Perplexity exactly what your page is about. Not keywords. Facts.

"This is a LocalBusiness. Named X. In Johannesburg. Open Monday to Friday. Offering these three services. At this price range."

AI models lean on structured data heavily when deciding who to cite. It is the difference between the AI guessing and the AI knowing.

The three types every SA business should have:

1. Organisation or LocalBusiness — name, address, phone, opening hours, service area
2. Service — for each thing you sell, with descriptions and price ranges where possible
3. FAQPage — on any page with a FAQ section. This is the single easiest win.

Test yours: search.google.com/test/rich-results — paste your URL, see what Google detects.

If it returns "No items detected", you are leaving AI-search visibility on the table. DM me your URL and I'll tell you the top 3 schema additions that would move the needle for your business.

#GEO #SchemaMarkup #SEO #SouthAfrica
```

## Wednesday — Case study / performance

```
A client came to us last year with a beautiful website that nobody was finding.

Lighthouse performance score: 32 on mobile. LCP: 6.1 seconds. Bounce rate from Google Business Profile visits: 71%.

Every second a mobile visitor waits above 3 seconds, around 20% of them leave. Six seconds means you've lost most of the room before they see a word.

Here's what we changed. No visual redesign:

— Moved hero imagery from 4MB JPEGs to WebP under 200KB
— Deferred every third-party script below the fold (chat widget, heatmap, analytics beacons)
— Switched from a heavy slider plugin to a static hero with one optimised image
— Fixed layout shift by declaring image dimensions explicitly
— Moved the site to static hosting with edge caching

New numbers: Lighthouse 96. LCP 1.4 seconds. Bounce rate 38%.

Enquiries per month doubled inside 60 days. Same content. Same SEO. Same brand. Just faster.

The site you already have is often the biggest untapped asset in the business. Not because it needs a redesign — because it needs a tune-up.

If your Lighthouse score on pagespeed.web.dev is below 70, drop me the URL. I'll tell you the top three things to fix.

#WebPerformance #CoreWebVitals #Johannesburg
```

## Friday — Thought leadership (AI automation)

```
"We should use AI" is one of the most expensive sentences a SA business owner can say without a plan behind it.

I had a call this month with a logistics company doing R80M revenue, ten people in ops. They'd bought four AI tools in 18 months. None of them were being used.

Here is what actually works, in my experience. Start with one repetitive process that eats a person's morning.

Not "transform customer service". Not "AI-powered everything". ONE process.

Three examples I've implemented this quarter:

1. Quote generation — sales team was writing quotes from scratch. We built a Claude-powered internal tool that takes scope notes and generates a branded quote PDF in 30 seconds. Saves 6-8 hours a week.

2. Email triage — a property firm's info@ inbox was drowning. A workflow now routes emails to the right team member with a draft reply already written. First-response time dropped from 2 days to 90 minutes.

3. Document extraction — a legal practice was spending hours pulling key data from scanned contracts. An automation now does it in seconds with 98% accuracy, humans verify.

The pattern: one process. Clear input. Clear output. Measurable time saved. Then move to the next one.

If you've got a repetitive task you wish would disappear, tell me what it is — happy to sanity-check whether AI would actually solve it or just add noise.

#AIAutomation #SmallBusiness #SouthAfrica
```
