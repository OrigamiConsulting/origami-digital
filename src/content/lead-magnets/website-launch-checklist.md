Your comprehensive, step-by-step checklist to ensure nothing falls through the cracks when launching (or relaunching) your business website. Print it out, tick it off, and launch with confidence.

---

## 1. Before You Build — Planning & Strategy

- [ ] Define 2-3 clear business goals for your website (e.g. generate leads, sell products, build credibility)
- [ ] Identify your target audience and create a simple customer profile
- [ ] Analyse 3-5 competitor websites — note what works and what you can do better
- [ ] Register a professional domain name (prefer .co.za for South African businesses)
- [ ] Choose reliable hosting or a deployment platform (e.g. Vercel, Netlify, or local SA hosting)
- [ ] Map out your site structure and key pages (Home, About, Services, Contact as a minimum)
- [ ] Plan your content strategy — what pages do you need and what will each one say?
- [ ] Set a realistic budget and timeline, including ongoing maintenance costs

---

## 2. Design & Development

- [ ] Design mobile-first — over 60% of South African web traffic is on mobile devices
- [ ] Ensure brand consistency: logo, colours, and fonts match across every page
- [ ] Optimise for page speed — compress images, minify code, use modern formats like WebP
- [ ] Meet basic accessibility standards (readable fonts, sufficient colour contrast, keyboard navigation)
- [ ] Install an SSL certificate so your site loads over HTTPS (non-negotiable for trust and SEO)
- [ ] Test all forms, buttons, and interactive elements before launch
- [ ] Ensure your site looks and works correctly on screens from 375px to 1920px and beyond
- [ ] Add a favicon and touch icons so your brand appears in browser tabs and bookmarks

---

## 3. SEO Essentials

- [ ] Write unique meta titles (50-60 characters) for every page, including your primary keyword
- [ ] Write unique meta descriptions (150-160 characters) for every page with a clear value proposition
- [ ] Use proper heading hierarchy — one H1 per page, H2s for sections, H3s for sub-sections
- [ ] Add descriptive alt text to every image (not just "image1.jpg")
- [ ] Generate and submit an XML sitemap to Google Search Console
- [ ] Create a robots.txt file that allows search engines to crawl your public pages
- [ ] Set up and verify your site in Google Search Console
- [ ] Add structured data (JSON-LD) for your organisation, services, and breadcrumbs

---

## 4. Content & Copy

- [ ] Craft a clear value proposition visible within 5 seconds of landing on your homepage
- [ ] Include a call-to-action (CTA) on every single page — never leave visitors without a next step
- [ ] Write an About page that tells your real story — people buy from people, not faceless brands
- [ ] Make your contact information visible on every page (footer at minimum, header ideally)
- [ ] Add a FAQ section addressing your customers' most common questions
- [ ] Proofread all copy for spelling and grammar — get a second pair of eyes on it
- [ ] Use South African English consistently (colour, not color; optimise, not optimize)
- [ ] Include social proof wherever possible — testimonials, client logos, case study results

---

## 5. Technical Launch

- [ ] Design a custom 404 error page that helps lost visitors find their way back
- [ ] Set up 301 redirects from any old URLs to their new equivalents (critical for site relaunches)
- [ ] Install Google Analytics 4 and verify it is tracking page views correctly
- [ ] Test every form end-to-end — submit, confirm the notification arrives, check the thank-you page
- [ ] Test your site in Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge on both desktop and mobile
- [ ] Run a speed test using Google PageSpeed Insights and address any critical issues
- [ ] Check that all internal and external links work (no broken links)
- [ ] Back up your site files and database before going live

---

## 6. Post-Launch

- [ ] Create and optimise your Google Business Profile with accurate address, hours, and services
- [ ] List your business in relevant South African directories (Yellow Pages SA, Brabys, etc.)
- [ ] Update all social media profiles with your new website URL
- [ ] Publish your first 2-3 blog posts to give search engines fresh content to index
- [ ] Set up email marketing — even a simple welcome sequence for new enquiries
- [ ] Monitor Google Search Console weekly for crawl errors and indexing issues
- [ ] Set up uptime monitoring so you know immediately if your site goes down
- [ ] Ask happy clients for Google reviews to build local search credibility

---

## 7. AI-Powered Extras — The Competitive Edge

- [ ] Add an AI chatbot to handle common visitor questions 24/7 (and capture leads after hours)
- [ ] Set up automated email sequences — enquiry acknowledgement, follow-up, and nurture
- [ ] Optimise your content for Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) — AI search engines like Google AI Mode and Perplexity
- [ ] Create an AI-assisted content calendar to maintain consistent publishing without the overwhelm
- [ ] Implement smart contact forms that adapt fields based on the service a visitor is interested in
- [ ] Use AI-powered analytics to identify which pages convert and which need attention
- [ ] Automate your social media posting with AI-generated drafts reviewed by a human
